K2; A Deadly Mountain

At 8,611m, K2 is the second-highest mountain on Earth. K2 is one of only 14 mountains in the world over 8,000m.

Learn about K2

According to Atlas and Boots, K2 got its name when in 1856 Thomas George Montgomerie – a British officer working for the Great Trigonometrical Survey of India – climbed a small mountain in Kashmir. He spotted two prominent peaks over 200km away in the Karakoram and named them K1 and K2, the ‘K’ standing for Karakoram. K1 was renamed Masherbrum – its local name. However, K2 appeared not to have a local name, possibly due to its remoteness.

Death Rate

 Pain
It is estimated that between one in four or five K2 summiteers die on the mountain. This is a summit-to-death rate of around 22%. On Everest, this rate is estimated to be 3%. Annapurna is the only eight-thousander with a higher death rate at around 25%.


Only 377 people have ever summitted K2, while in comparison, Everest has had over 4000 people summit.

The 2008 K2 Disaster

The 2008 K2 disaster was the deadliest day in the history of K2 mountaineering. Eleven mountaineers died in a series of incidents following an ice avalanche that swept away fixed ropes near a feature called the Bottleneck.
